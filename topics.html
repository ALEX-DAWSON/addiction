What are some in home skills I can teach my child to cope better?

Maya Kalan-McPhee

You can learn to cope with the addictions and difficult emotions to manage. This can help you only if you let it. One of the core skills is Wise Mind. This skill teaches you to be in the moment and understand the consequences of your actions. Sometimes this skill may seem stupid or unworthy of your time due to the time it takes to be able to do it independently. To be completely honest, you’re never going to be “perfect”, but who is right? This skill falls under a program specified for mental health called “DBT”. “Dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) is a specific type of cognitive-behavioral psychotherapy developed in the late 1980s by psychologist Marsha M. Linehan to help better treat borderline personality disorder. Since its development, it has also been used for the treatment of other kinds of mental health disorders”.

What Has a Positive Impact on Drug Users?

Maya Kalan-McPhee

First off you first need to start by getting there consent for whatever your doing with them. Then you should sit them down to brainstorm ideas for your start of the journey of positive impact. Some things that may have a negative impact are taking their drugs away completely and being harsh or yelling at them. Discipline is helpful but you would need to lean them off of the drug and not completely confiscate the drug or things could become negative and could damage the relationship. There are many ways to start to become a positive role model for the drug user, like staying away from drugs yourself or relating their situation to a similar one of your own. It could be in past tense or could be in present tense, but try not to relate it to anything that could make the person become emotional. There are many ways to becoming confident and being emotionally stable. Some of which are meditating, deep breathing and trying to push other people's judgements aside. You can start by going into the conversation with an open mind and not judging the other person for their ideas . They may say things you don't agree with but again don't shut them down ,consider it and try to incorporate their ideas into your own. Ideas they may have for stopping the addiction and or what they think has a positive impact of them all vary due to individual needs. So one person might need something like a teddy bear to cuddle and others may need the drug taken away and handle the withdrawal symptoms.

Jeffery Cove

Some things that will have a positive impact on other drug user or ex-drug users could be keeping them distracted and trying to do more things. Even going for walks could have a positive impact because it keeps them distracted. Also not leaving the person alone because that could make them think about it and then they may end up going back in the drugs. Some other things you could do for the person is trying to get them into a good hobby. For example, you could get them into an art class, a book club, or help them get back any friends that may have left them during their addiction. I wouldn't suggest any sports because that would be a lot for someone to handle when going through addiction recovery. Aswell, a lot of people that are drug users aren't very active. Lastly, I think that a positive thing for the person with an addiction is keeping them away from stress or anything that will make them think about the drug. Keep them away from anyone that does drugs or has any access to the drug because then the person will end up getting addicted to that drug again. If that happens, then you will have to start over with recovery from the person's addiction.
