Alcohol

Jeffrey Cove

Some symptoms of withdrawals are headaches, heart palpitations, seizures, anxiety, confusion, and restlessness. Not all people have these symptoms, but it is most common for people to have them. These happen because when you drink alcohol frequently or heavily, it starts to slow down your brain. Then your body starts producing more chemicals and it starts to keep producing them, even when you are not drinking alcohol. Most of these symptoms happen because alcohol affects your dopamine. Dopamine regulates your energy and motivation

Tobacco

Jeffrey Cove

Not everyone will experience nicotine withdrawals, but people that quit cold turkey will be affected the most. But if you slowly quit smoking, you will have less nicotine withdrawal. Some withdrawal symptoms are nicotine cravings, snacking and weight gain, sleep disturbance, persistent cough, flu like symptoms, and mood changes. Most people gain about 10 pounds when quitting tobacco because people tend to replace smoking with food. So whenever a person has a craving, they just eat something to make it go away or distract them from the craving. Most people think when they get a persistent cough then they are getting worse but this actually means your lungs are getting better and making you cough up the bad stuff in your lungs.

Opioids

Jeffrey Cove

Symptoms for opioid withdrawals include runny nose, teary eyes, restlessness, insomnia, uncontrollable yawning, excessive sweating, anxiety, and muscle spasms. Some more intense symptoms are rapid heart rate, high blood pressure, dilated pupils, perinioya, vomiting, and diarrhea. To help cope with these withdrawal symptoms you can take hot baths, exercise, yoga, do stuff that entertains you, and keep hydrated.
